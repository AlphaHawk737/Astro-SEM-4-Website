The Evidence for the Unseen

Dark matter didn’t start as some dramatic sci-fi idea. It started as an accounting problem.

 

In the 1930s, Fritz Zwicky studied galaxy clusters and found something strange.

 

The galaxies were moving too fast to be held together by visible matter alone.

 

Decades later, Vera Rubin and Kent Ford observed the same issue inside galaxies.

 

They didn’t.
